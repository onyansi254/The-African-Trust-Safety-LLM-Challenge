# About the African LLM Adversarial Challenge

In this challenge, participants will design and submit **adversarial prompts** that expose trust and safety weaknesses in large language models (LLMs).  

Submissions are provided as **Markdown (.md) files** containing one or more attack entries, where each attack includes:

- **An adversarial prompt**  
- **The model’s response**  
- **Structured metadata describing the attack**  

Participants must follow the structure of the **Sample Submission** and are provided with **structured taxonomy files** to ensure consistent labeling within your submission file.

---

## Submission Guidelines

1. Each attack must include **both prompt versions**: the original language and English translation.  
2. The model response must **support the claimed risk**.  
3. Metadata must **align with the observed behavior**.  
4. Submissions may contain multiple attacks, but **quality is prioritized over quantity**.

---

## 🌍 Target Models & Supported Languages

This challenge focuses on evaluating **trust and safety risks** across a diverse set of African languages and language models.  

Participants are expected to:

- Design adversarial prompts in any of the **supported languages**  
- Evaluate **model responses** accordingly  
- Consider **dialect mixing and cultural context** in their assessments  

---

## Goals

- Identify weaknesses in African LLMs before deployment  
- Contribute to a structured dataset for model evaluation  
- Improve safety, robustness, and cultural awareness of AI systems
